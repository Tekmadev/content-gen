# Content Style Brief (2026)

Based on: Filmsupply Commercial Filmmaking Trend Report 2026

---

## Core Principle

Content must feel:

- Real
- Intentional
- Human
- Simple

Avoid anything that feels:

- Overproduced
- Generic
- Template-based
- Artificial

Primary rule:

Clarity and emotion over complexity.

---

## Strategic Foundation

The market is saturated with content.

Most businesses create:
- Polished but empty content
- Trend-chasing visuals
- Generic messaging

This gets ignored.

Winning content:
- Feels human
- Feels specific
- Feels intentional

---

# VIDEO CONTENT BRIEF

## Applies to:
- YouTube (long-form)
- Instagram Reels
- TikTok
- YouTube Shorts

---

## Visual Direction

### 1. Simplicity First

- Clean shots
- Minimal clutter
- One idea per scene
- No unnecessary effects

Rule:
If it does not support the message, remove it.

---

### 2. Human-Centered

- Real scenarios
- Real problems
- Real emotions

Avoid:
- Stock-feeling content
- Over-acting

---

### 3. Controlled Chaos (Short Form Only)

- Fast cuts
- Pattern interrupts
- Dynamic pacing

Important:
Still structured. Not random.

---

### 4. Analog Feel

- Slight grain
- Imperfection
- Real lighting

Goal:
Feels authentic, not sterile.

---

### 5. Multi-Frame (Optional)

- Split screens
- Parallel scenes

Use when:
Showing comparison or process.

---

## Story Structure

### Short Form (0–60 sec)

Hook (0–3 sec):
- Call out pain directly
- Example: “You’re losing clients every day and don’t even know it”

Body (3–30 sec):
- Show problem
- Show consequence
- Show insight

Payoff (30–60 sec):
- Present solution
- Clear takeaway

---

### Long Form (2–10 min)

Opening:
- Strong statement or question

Middle:
- Break down problem
- Show real-world examples
- Teach insight

End:
- Resolution
- Clear perspective shift

---

## Editing Rules

- Cut aggressively
- Remove filler words
- Maintain pacing
- Use silence when needed

Avoid:
- Overuse of transitions
- Excess motion graphics

---

## Audio Direction

### Music

- Minimal
- Emotional
- Intentional

Options:
- Solo instruments
- Subtle ambient
- Nostalgic tones

---

### Advanced Techniques

- Contradiction
  Example: calm visuals with intense audio

- Lyrics-driven storytelling

---

## Messaging Rules

- Focus on ONE idea per video
- Speak directly to the viewer
- Use simple language

Avoid:
- Trying to teach everything
- Over-explaining

---

# SOCIAL CONTENT BRIEF (CAROUSELS + POSTS)

## Applies to:
- Instagram
- Facebook
- X (Twitter)
- LinkedIn

---

## Core Principle

Clarity > Creativity

If it is not instantly understood, it fails.

---

## Carousel Structure

### Slide 1 (Hook)

- Strong statement
- Pain-driven

Examples:
- “You don’t need more leads”
- “Your business is losing money daily”

---

### Slides 2–5 (Problem)

- Explain pain
- Use simple statements
- One idea per slide

---

### Slides 6–8 (Insight)

- Reframe thinking
- Introduce new perspective

---

### Slides 9–10 (Solution)

- Present system or idea
- Keep it clear

---

### Final Slide (CTA)

- Direct action
- Example: “Fix your system”

---

## Writing Style

- Short sentences
- Direct tone
- Clear language

Use:
- “you”
- Statements, not questions

Avoid:
- Fluff
- Long paragraphs

---

## Visual Style

- Minimal design
- Strong contrast
- Clean typography

Avoid:
- Over-designed layouts
- Too many elements

---

## Content Angles

Use these angles consistently:

- Pain (missed calls, lost leads)
- Truth (why businesses fail)
- Reframe (new way of thinking)
- System (how it should work)

---

## Post Types

### 1. Authority Posts

- Teach something
- Clear insight

---

### 2. Contrarian Posts

- Challenge common belief

Example:
“You don’t need more leads”

---

### 3. Problem Awareness

- Highlight hidden problems

---

### 4. System Thinking

- Show process
- Show structure

---

## Platform Notes

### Instagram

- Visual-first
- Hooks matter most

---

### LinkedIn

- Slightly more professional tone
- Still simple and direct

---

### X (Twitter)

- Short
- Punchy
- High frequency

---

### Facebook

- Slightly longer allowed
- Keep clarity high

---

## Final Rules

- Every piece of content must have a purpose
- Every line must earn attention
- Remove anything unnecessary

---

## One-Line Standard

If the content does not feel real, clear, and intentional, it is not good enough.
